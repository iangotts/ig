LYRICS: Until Morale Improves

[Verse 1]
He arrived in a black car with a cheque the size of God,
bought the kingdom out from under them with a smile and a nod.
The founders — the beloved, every conference knew their names —
he walked them to the exit while the cameras caught the flames.
Then he crowned the ones who stayed, said "the throne is yours, my dears" —
and handed them a velvet collar and a lifetime of arrears.

[Pre-Chorus]
By the morning it's the revenue — cut deeper, grow it fast —
by the evening it's the genius in the code that's gonna last.
He turns just like the weather, and the weather wants you dead;
más y más, more and more is the only prayer he's said.

[Chorus]
Until morale improves, the beatings carry on —
he'll adore you in the evening and he'll fire you by the dawn.
It's revenue tonight, but it's the IP by first light —
he'll burn it all for love and swear the ashes are alright.

[Verse 2]
The morale's down in the gutter where he kicked it for a thrill;
he withholds the very water, then he wonders why they're ill.
"Sack him." Then "I want him back." Then "who told you that he's gone?" —
every order is a trapdoor and the floor keeps moving on.
He micromanages heaven into something close to hell,
counts the pennies, loses kingdoms, rings the same old funeral bell.

[Chorus]
Until morale improves, the beatings carry on —
he'll adore you in the evening and he'll fire you by the dawn.
It's revenue tonight, but it's the IP by first light —
he'll burn it all for love and swear the ashes are alright.

[Bridge — El Rey de Ceniza]
He bought himself a kingdom just to set it all alight,
paid a king's own ransom for the pleasure of the fight.
Now the throne is only embers and the crown is only debt —
el rey de ceniza — the richest pauper yet.
A fraction of a fortune, all that fury for a coin;
he destroyed the thing he died for, kissed the blade and called it joy.

[Final Chorus]
Until morale improves, the beatings carry on —
he'll adore you in the evening and he'll fire you by the dawn.
It's revenue tonight, but it's the IP by first light —
he'll burn it all for love and swear the ashes are alright.

[Outro]
Sell more, spend less, mi amor —
burn the whole house down, then ask what it was for.
(Until morale improves… until morale improves…)
